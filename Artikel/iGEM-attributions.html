Attributions

Outside of the team roster

Göttingen Genomics Laboratory

RNA sequencing

For acquiring RNA sequencing data, we asked Professor Rolf Daniel from Göttingen Genomics Laboratory (G2L) to support our project. He provided us a lot of RNA sequencing data that enabled us to overthink our detection method with reporter genes.

ITC measurements

For isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC) measurements and structure elucidation of our purified proteins AroA from Escherichia coli and AroE from Bacillus subtilis we sent our samples to the Bange lab in Marburg. Gert Bange is also supervising an iGEM team this year and will hopefully provide us with data? TO DO

Seqlab Göttingen

DNA sequencing

To acquire DNA sequences, we sent our samples as described in the notebook section to Microsynth Seqlab, based in Göttingen.

Detection of glyphosate

For the detection of glyphosate, we sent our samples to the Ischebeck lab in Göttingen, which provided us with the possibility to use their gas chromatography and mass spectrometry devices for determining the concentration of glyphosate in samples. TO DO

Genes

We ordered some genes, called g-Blocks, from Integrated DNA Technologies, which was free, since they sponsor the iGEM competition.
